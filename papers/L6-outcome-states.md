# L6 – Outcome States: Stability, Collapse, and System Attractors

## Abstract

This paper analyzes how systems converge toward stable or unstable states based on their internal coherence, circulation, and functional alignment.

Zanistarast defines this as **L6 – Outcome States**,
the final condition toward which systems evolve.

---

## 1. Introduction

All systems evolve over time.

They do not remain neutral.

They move toward:

- stability  
or  
- collapse  

Zanistarast models this as:

> L6 – Outcome States

---

## 2. Physical Systems

In physics:

- systems tend toward stable configurations
- unstable states decay

Examples:

- atoms stabilize at low-energy states
- unstable particles decay
- systems move toward equilibrium or breakdown

Thus:

> Physical systems converge toward attractors.

---

## 3. Biological Systems

In biology:

- organisms maintain homeostasis
- failure leads to disease or death

Examples:

- stable metabolism → survival
- imbalance → system failure
- ecosystem collapse when thresholds are crossed

This shows:

> Biological systems converge toward viability or collapse.

---

## 4. Computational Systems

In computing:

- programs either execute successfully
or  
- fail

Examples:

- successful execution → valid output
- runtime error → crash
- corrupted state → undefined behavior

Thus:

> Systems terminate in defined states.

---

## 5. Economic and Social Systems

In human systems:

- stable systems maintain order
- unstable systems degrade

Examples:

- sustainable economies → long-term growth
- imbalance → crisis
- governance failure → systemic collapse

Thus:

> Systems evolve toward stability or breakdown.

---

## 6. Zanistarast Interpretation

Zanistarast defines:

> L6 – Outcome States = The terminal condition of a system

This condition depends on:

- L2 (validation)
- L3 (circulation)
- L5 (alignment)

If these are satisfied:

→ system stabilizes

If these fail:

→ system collapses

---

## 7. Cross-Domain Convergence

Outcome states appear in:

- physics → stable vs unstable states
- biology → life vs failure
- computing → success vs crash
- society → order vs collapse

Zanistarast models this as a universal pattern.

---

## 8. Hypothesis

Systems that:

- maintain coherence (L2)
- sustain circulation (L3)
- preserve alignment (L5)

→ converge toward stability

Systems that fail in these:

→ converge toward collapse

---

## 9. Attractor Model

Zanistarast suggests:

- systems evolve toward attractors
- attractors represent stable configurations
- instability leads to divergence or failure

---

## 10. Conclusion

Systems do not remain undefined.

They resolve into:

- stable states  
or  
- unstable outcomes  

Zanistarast formalizes this as:

> L6 – Outcome States
