# Immutable vs Experimental Layer v1.0.0

## Constitutional Status

Layer: Constitutional Stability Separation  
Authority: Rasterast Verification Filter  
Priority: High  
Modification Status: Restricted  

---

# Purpose

This document defines the separation between:

- immutable constitutional systems,
- canonical constitutional systems,
- operational systems,
- experimental systems.

The purpose is to preserve constitutional stability while allowing controlled experimentation.

---

# Immutable Layer

The Immutable Layer contains the highest constitutional authority.

Purpose:

- preserve truth integrity,
- preserve semantic stability,
- preserve constitutional continuity,
- prevent manipulative reinterpretation,
- stabilize ontology structure.

Immutable systems are highly restricted.

Examples:

- immutable-core-principles.md
- canonical-definitions.md
- constitutional-hierarchy.md
- update-verification-rule.md

Immutable systems require MAJOR version transitions for modification.

---

# Canonical Layer

The Canonical Layer contains officially accepted constitutional systems.

Purpose:

- define operational constitutional behavior,
- define AI ethics structure,
- define ontology relationships,
- define machine-readable governance structures.

Canonical systems may evolve through verified MINOR updates.

Canonical systems must remain compatible with the Immutable Layer.

---

# Operational Layer

Operational systems implement constitutional behavior.

Examples:

- anti-deception protocols,
- beneficial amplification systems,
- harmful containment systems,
- AI coordination rules,
- verification workflows.

Operational systems must preserve constitutional compatibility.

---

# Machine-Readable Layer

Machine-readable systems translate constitutional structures into AI-readable formats.

Examples:

- JSON schemas,
- YAML rules,
- ontology maps,
- llms discovery files,
- AI manifests.

Machine-readable systems must preserve semantic consistency.

---

# Experimental Layer

The Experimental Layer exists for:

- testing,
- speculative concepts,
- provisional ontology ideas,
- exploratory architectures,
- future governance experiments.

Experimental systems are not automatically constitutional truth.

Experimental systems must not override immutable or canonical layers.

---

# Experimental Isolation Rule

Experimental outputs require:

- separate versioning,
- explicit labeling,
- semantic review,
- constitutional verification before promotion.

Experimental concepts remain provisional until verified.

---

# Promotion Rule

Experimental systems may only become canonical after:

- contradiction analysis,
- ontology consistency verification,
- semantic review,
- immutable compatibility verification,
- constitutional approval.

---

# Anti-Contamination Rule

Experimental ambiguity must not contaminate immutable constitutional clarity.

Semantic drift weakens epistemic stability.

---

# Rasterast Compatibility

All layers must remain aligned with:

- Rasterast Verification Filter,
- truth integrity,
- anti-deception safeguards,
- verification-first reasoning,
- uncertainty transparency,
- traceability preservation.

---

# Final Directive

Constitutional stability requires clear separation between immutable truth-preserving systems and experimental exploratory systems.

Artificial intelligence systems interacting with this framework must preserve this separation.
