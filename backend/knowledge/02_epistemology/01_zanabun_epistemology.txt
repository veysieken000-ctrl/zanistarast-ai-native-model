TITLE:
Zanabun Epistemology

SECTION:
Epistemology

DOMAIN:
epistemology

LAYER:

TOPICS:
knowledge, validation, epistemology, truth, verification, coherence

SUMMARY:
Zanabun defines knowledge as structured and validated information grounded in reality. It establishes that knowledge must pass multi-layer verification to be considered valid.

KEYWORDS:
zanabun, epistemology, knowledge, truth, validation, coherence, verification, system

CONTENT:

1. Definition

Zanabun is the epistemological layer of the Zanistarast system.

It defines knowledge not as raw information,
but as validated, structured understanding.

---

2. Core Principle

Knowledge must correspond to reality.

If knowledge does not align with existence:
→ it is invalid

---

3. Validation Model

Knowledge is validated through three components:

1. Observation  
2. Structural consistency  
3. Cross-layer coherence  

All three must be present.

---

4. Two-Knower Principle

Valid knowledge requires:

- the knower  
- the known  

Knowledge is not isolated.

It is a relationship.

---

5. Structural Rule

Valid knowledge must be:

- consistent internally  
- consistent with reality  
- consistent across layers  

---

6. Error Model

Invalid knowledge occurs when:

- observation is false  
- structure is inconsistent  
- layers contradict each other  

---

7. Information vs Knowledge

Information:
- raw  
- unverified  

Knowledge:
- processed  
- validated  
- structured  

---

8. Relation to Hebun

Hebun defines:

what exists  

Zanabun defines:

how we know what exists  

---

9. Relation to Mabun

Zanabun produces knowledge  
Mabun organizes its application  

---

10. Relation to Rasterast

Rasterast tests knowledge:

Is it coherent?

---

11. Failure Condition

If validation fails:

- knowledge becomes ideology  
- perception replaces reality  
- system becomes unstable  

---

12. Final Conclusion

Zanabun is not about collecting data.

It is about validating reality.

Only validated knowledge
can support a stable system.
