# ZANABÛN — Epistemological Structure of Knowledge

Zanabûn is the epistemological layer of the Zanistarast system. It defines how knowledge is formed, validated, structured, and sustained across different layers of reality.

Zanabûn rejects both naive empiricism and purely abstract reasoning. Knowledge is not merely observation, nor is it detached logic. It is structured validation across layers.

## Core Principle: Dual-Knower Validation

The central principle of Zanabûn is the "Dual-Knower" model:

A valid piece of knowledge must satisfy two conditions:

1. Observational Grounding  
The knowledge must be connected to observable or measurable phenomena.

2. Structural Consistency  
The knowledge must remain coherent across ontological layers.

If one of these fails, the knowledge is incomplete or invalid.

## Knowledge as Structured Process

Zanabûn defines knowledge as a function:

Knowledge = f(Being)

This means knowledge is not independent. It emerges from the structure of existence defined in Hebûn.

Thus:
- invalid ontology → invalid knowledge  
- inconsistent structure → unstable knowledge  

## Rejection of Isolated Models

Zanabûn rejects:

- Pure empiricism (data without structure)  
- Pure rationalism (logic without grounding)  
- Ideological systems (belief without validation)  

All of these fail because they break the dual validation requirement.

## Layer-Based Knowledge

Knowledge must be valid across layers:

- Physical knowledge → must respect physical laws  
- Biological knowledge → must respect life systems  
- Mental knowledge → must align with cognition  
- Ethical knowledge → must reflect responsibility  
- Civilizational knowledge → must scale structurally  

A knowledge claim valid in only one layer is insufficient.

## Validation Mechanism

Zanabûn evaluates knowledge through:

- Ontological compatibility (Hebûn alignment)  
- Internal logical consistency  
- Cross-layer stability  
- Reproducibility (where applicable)  

If a claim fails in any of these, it is filtered out.

## Against Positivism

Zanabûn differs from classical positivist science:

Positivism:
- focuses on observable data  
- often ignores structural and ontological depth  

Zanabûn:
- includes observation  
- but requires structural coherence  

Thus, Zanabûn expands science rather than rejecting it.

## Knowledge Stability

A knowledge system is stable if:

- it does not collapse under cross-layer analysis  
- it maintains consistency under expansion  
- it produces reliable predictions or structures  

Unstable knowledge leads to systemic failure in higher layers.

## Role in the System

In Zanistarast:

- Hebûn defines what exists  
- Zanabûn defines how it is known  

Without Zanabûn:
- knowledge becomes fragmented  
- truth becomes relative  
- systems become unreliable  

## Logical Consequence

If knowledge must satisfy dual validation, then:

- belief is not knowledge  
- data alone is not knowledge  
- coherence alone is not knowledge  

Only structured, validated, and consistent knowledge survives.

## Epistemological Depth

Zanabûn does not ask only:

“What do we know?”

It asks:

- how do we validate knowledge  
- how does knowledge remain stable  
- how does knowledge scale across systems  

Thus, Zanabûn is not descriptive epistemology.  
It is structural epistemology.

## Final Statement

Knowledge is not accumulation.  
It is validation under structure.

Zanabûn is the system that ensures knowledge remains grounded, coherent, and stable across all layers of existence.
