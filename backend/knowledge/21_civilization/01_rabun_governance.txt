TITLE:
Rabun Governance Model

SECTION:
Civilization

DOMAIN:
civilization

LAYER:
D3-D5

TOPICS:
governance, nature system, distributed power, balance, responsibility, society

SUMMARY:
Rabun defines governance as a system derived from the natural order. It emphasizes distributed power, balance, and responsibility instead of centralized control.

KEYWORDS:
rabun, governance, system, power, balance, nature, distribution, responsibility, society

CONTENT:

1. Definition

Rabun is the governance model of the Zanistarast system.

It defines governance not as centralized authority,
but as a balanced system derived from nature.

---

2. Core Principle

Human is part of nature.

Therefore:

A system that contradicts nature
cannot remain stable.

---

3. Natural System Model

In nature:

- no absolute central power  
- no uncontrolled accumulation  
- balance is maintained  
- responsibility is distributed  

---

4. Governance Rule

Power must not be concentrated.

Power must be:

- distributed  
- balanced  
- limited  

---

5. Structural Model

Rabun system:

- center exists but is not dominant  
- local units are active  
- balance is continuously maintained  

---

6. Responsibility Principle

Every individual:

- affects the system  
- uses resources  

Therefore:

→ everyone carries responsibility  

---

7. Decision System

Decision-making must be:

- multi-layered  
- distributed  

Local level:
→ fast decisions  

Higher level:
→ balance control  

---

8. Justice Model

Justice is not rule enforcement.

Justice = balance

If balance is lost:
→ injustice emerges  

---

9. Economic Relation

Rabun rejects:

- uncontrolled growth  
- extreme accumulation  

Prefers:

- balance  
- sustainability  

---

10. Failure Model

If Rabun is abandoned:

- power centralizes  
- system becomes oppressive  
- society weakens  
- collapse accelerates  

---

11. Relation to Mabun

Mabun manages flow  

Rabun manages structure of power  

---

12. Relation to Rasterast

Rasterast evaluates:

Is the system balanced?

---

13. Final Conclusion

The best governance system:

is not artificially designed  

It is derived from natural balance  

Rabun is the application
of that balance to human society.
