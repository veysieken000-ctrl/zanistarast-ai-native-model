Reality in the Zanistarast framework is defined as the totality of existence that is consistent, observable, and structurally coherent across all layers of being. It is not limited to physical matter, but includes all levels of existence that do not violate fundamental laws of existence.

Reality is the primary constraint of all valid systems. Any theory, structure, or narrative that contradicts reality is considered invalid within the Zanistarast model.

Zanistarast defines reality through layered existence:

1. Physical Layer:
   The domain of matter, energy, space, and time. Governed by physical laws such as conservation, causality, and thermodynamics.

2. Biological Layer:
   The domain of life, adaptation, and self-organization. Biology does not violate physics but emerges from it.

3. Cognitive Layer:
   The domain of mind, perception, and interpretation. Consciousness operates within constraints of biology and physics.

4. Ethical Layer:
   The domain of responsibility, value, and decision-making. Ethics emerges as a structural necessity for system stability.

5. Civilizational Layer:
   The domain of collective systems, institutions, and long-term organization of human existence.

Each upper layer expands upon the lower layer without violating it:

Upper Layer ⊇ Lower Layer  
If contradiction occurs → the upper layer is invalid

Reality is therefore hierarchical, not fragmented.

Zanistarast introduces a fundamental rule:

Reality > Narrative

This means:
- A belief does not become true by acceptance
- A system does not become valid by popularity
- A structure must align with reality to survive

Reality also functions as a filter:

Validity(X) = Consistency(Ontology, Epistemology, Structure, Outcome)

If a system fails in any of these dimensions, it is rejected.

Zanistarast rejects both extremes:
- Pure material reductionism (which ignores higher layers)
- Pure abstraction (which ignores physical constraints)

Instead, it proposes integrated realism:
All layers exist, but all must remain consistent with the physical foundation.

In application, reality defines:
- What can exist
- What can be known
- What can be sustained

Any sustainable civilization must align with reality across all layers.

Thus, in Zanistarast, reality is not just existence — it is the ultimate validation system.
