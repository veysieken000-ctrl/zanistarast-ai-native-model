Axiology in Zanistarast is the structured study of value, meaning, and hierarchical worth within a coherent and reality-aligned system. It is not merely a philosophical inquiry into "what is good," but a systemic evaluation of value based on ontological grounding (Hebûn), epistemological validation (Zanabûn), structural responsibility (Mabûn), and consistency filtering (Rasterast).

In the Zanistarast framework, value is not subjective or arbitrary. Instead, value emerges as a function of alignment with reality, structural coherence, and ethical responsibility. Therefore:

Value = f(Reality Alignment, Structural Coherence, Ethical Responsibility)

Axiology operates as a bridge between knowledge and action. It determines what should be prioritized, preserved, or rejected within a civilization model. Without a valid axiological structure, knowledge becomes directionless and power becomes unstable.

Zanistarast introduces a layered value system:

1. Ontological Value:
   The worth of a thing based on its alignment with existence itself (Hebûn). Anything that contradicts fundamental existence is considered valueless.

2. Epistemic Value:
   The worth of knowledge based on its verification, consistency, and reproducibility (Zanabûn). Knowledge that cannot be validated structurally loses its value.

3. Structural Value:
   The contribution of an element to system stability and sustainability (Mabûn). Value is measured by how much it strengthens the system.

4. Ethical Value:
   The responsibility and moral alignment of actions within a system. Ethics in Zanistarast is not abstract but structurally necessary for long-term stability.

5. Civilizational Value:
   The capacity of ideas, actions, and systems to sustain and advance a civilization (Zanistarast synthesis layer).

Axiology also defines value degradation:

- If narrative > structure → value collapses
- If power > truth → value becomes unstable
- If structure > reality → system becomes artificial and eventually fails

Thus, only values that survive all layers of consistency remain valid.

In application, axiology guides decision-making, governance, scientific prioritization, and cultural evolution. It ensures that systems are not driven by ideology, but by verifiable and structurally consistent principles.

Ultimately, Zanistarast axiology defines value as that which sustains truth, structure, and civilization simultaneously.
