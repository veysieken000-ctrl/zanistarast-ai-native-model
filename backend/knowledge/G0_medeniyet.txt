Civilization in the Zanistarast framework is defined as a structured, multi-layered system that organizes human existence in alignment with reality, knowledge, responsibility, and ethical coherence.

A civilization is not merely a collection of people, institutions, or cultural elements. It is a dynamic system that must maintain stability across all layers of existence.

Zanistarast defines civilization as:

Civilization = f(Reality, Knowledge, Structure, Ethics)

A civilization is considered valid and sustainable only if it satisfies all four components simultaneously.

1. Reality Alignment:
   A civilization must operate in accordance with the laws of reality. Any system that contradicts physical, biological, or cognitive constraints will eventually collapse.

2. Knowledge Integrity:
   Knowledge within a civilization must be verified, consistent, and transferable. False or unverified knowledge leads to systemic instability.

3. Structural Coherence:
   Institutions, systems, and infrastructures must function in a coordinated and sustainable manner. Fragmented or contradictory structures weaken civilization.

4. Ethical Responsibility:
   Ethics is not optional. It is a structural requirement. A civilization that ignores responsibility will lose stability over time.

Zanistarast introduces the concept of Civilization Stability:

Civilization Stability = f(Reality Alignment, Structural Consistency, Ethical Responsibility)

Instability arises when:
- Narrative replaces reality
- Power replaces truth
- Structure becomes inconsistent
- Ethics is ignored

Zanistarast also defines civilization layers:

- Individual Layer (self-awareness and responsibility)
- Social Layer (relationships and cooperation)
- Institutional Layer (governance, education, economy)
- Civilizational Layer (long-term structure and continuity)

Each layer must remain consistent with the others.

Zanistarast rejects:
- Systems driven purely by ideology
- Civilizations built on unverified beliefs
- Structures that prioritize power over truth

Instead, it proposes:

A Truth-Centered Civilization Model

Where:
- Knowledge is verified
- Structure is coherent
- Ethics is enforced through system design
- Power is constrained by truth

Civilization is not static.

It evolves based on:
- Knowledge expansion
- Structural adaptation
- Ethical refinement

Zanistarast views civilization as an engineering problem:

A system that can be designed, tested, improved, and stabilized.

Thus, civilization is not accidental.

It is constructed.

And only systems aligned with reality can endure.
