# ZANISTARAST: COLLAPSE MECHANISM (02_COLLAPSE_MECHANISM)

1. WHY IS COLLAPSE A MECHANISM?
According to Zanistarast science, collapse is not a chaotic extinction. It is a mandatory regulatory mechanism that occurs when a system reaches its capacity limit, clearing space for a higher dimension and resetting unbalanced growth.

2. THE LAW OF LOWER DIMENSION LIMITS:
Each dimension expands within itself: Physics -> Matter densifies; Biology -> Life diversity increases; Mind -> Information production accelerates. However, a lower dimension cannot expand infinitely. When limits of energy balance, complexity, and adaptation are exceeded, the system becomes unstable.

3. STEPS OF THE MECHANISM'S OPERATION:
The Collapse mechanism operates in four stages:
A) Excessive Growth: Diversity and power increase rapidly.
B) Balance Disturbance: Internal contradictions are produced within the system.
C) Loss of Stability: Large-scale fractures and breakdowns occur.
D) Reset: A new, simplified order is formed.
This cycle follows the path: Expansion -> Saturation -> Collapse -> Reconstruction.

4. COLLAPSE IN THE MIND DIMENSION:
When information increases, power densifies. Before the Moral dimension opens, if information and power are left without control, they turn into a crisis. Collapse at this stage is what makes the capacity for ethics mandatory.

5. BIOLOGICAL EXAMPLE (ZANISTARAST INTERPRETATION):
The mass extinctions seen in geological records are not biological failures, but preparations for a new living order. For example: After the peak of the Dinosaurs, the rise of flexible species shows that Collapse is an investment in the future.

6. COLLAPSE = CAPACITY UPDATE:
Collapse is not a punishment; it is a capacity update. The system leaves behind the complexity it can no longer carry and simplifies for the sake of the new dimension.

7. ZANISTARAST CONCLUSION:
The collapse mechanism is the primary safety system of the universe. Because uncontrolled growth produces total destruction, a controlled collapse produces a change in direction. Therefore: Collapse is not extinction, but a shift in orientation.
