TITLE:
Rasterast Method

SECTION:
Method

DOMAIN:
method

LAYER:
D1-D5

TOPICS:
coherence, validation, method, consistency, error detection, system stability

SUMMARY:
Rasterast is the coherence-based validation method of the Zanistarast system. It evaluates whether a concept, model, or system is consistent across ontology, epistemology, structure, and outcomes.

KEYWORDS:
rasterast, method, coherence, consistency, validation, system, error detection

CONTENT:

1. Definition

Rasterast is the methodological layer of the Zanistarast system.

It defines how systems are evaluated
in terms of coherence and consistency.

---

2. Core Principle

Validity = Coherence

If a system is not coherent:
→ it is invalid

---

3. Evaluation Model

Rasterast evaluates four layers:

1. Ontology (Hebun)  
2. Epistemology (Zanabun)  
3. Structure (Mabun)  
4. Outcome (Result)

---

4. Core Formula

Validity(X) = Coherence(Ontology, Epistemology, Structure, Outcome)

---

5. Coherence Rules

A system is valid only if:

- it matches reality (ontology)  
- it is correctly known (epistemology)  
- it is structurally balanced (Mabun)  
- it produces stable results  

---

6. Error Detection

A system is invalid if:

- it contradicts reality  
- it contains internal inconsistencies  
- it produces unstable outcomes  

---

7. Critical Heuristics

Rasterast defines:

If narrative > structure  
→ reject  

If structure > reality  
→ collapse  

Only coherence survives  

---

8. Structural Filtering

Rasterast filters:

- false theories  
- inconsistent systems  
- unstable models  

---

9. Relation to Hebun

Hebun defines existence  

Rasterast checks:

Is the model aligned with existence?

---

10. Relation to Zanabun

Zanabun defines knowledge  

Rasterast checks:

Is the knowledge valid?

---

11. Relation to Mabun

Mabun defines structure  

Rasterast checks:

Is the structure balanced?

---

12. Failure Condition

If coherence is lost:

- system becomes unstable  
- contradictions increase  
- collapse becomes inevitable  

---

13. Final Conclusion

Rasterast is not a theory.

It is a filter.

Only coherent systems
can survive and remain valid.
