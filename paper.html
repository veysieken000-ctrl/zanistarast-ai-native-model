Zanistarast Paper

A structured paper presenting the framework, its logic, and its cross-domain scientific position.

Abstract

Zanistarast is a structured framework for modeling natural, computational, and human systems. It proposes that coherent civilization depends on valid ontology, reliable epistemology, structural consistency, and interpretable integration.

1. Problem Statement

Modern fragmentation produces systems that lose coherence between reality, knowledge, structure, and legitimacy. When validation breaks, collapse begins at the epistemic and structural level.

2. Core Thesis

A sustainable framework must connect being, knowledge, method, and structure through a testable integrative model. Zanistarast proposes such a model.

3. Hebûn

The ontological layer. It defines being, existence, and the ground conditions of reality.

4. Zanabûn

The epistemological layer. It explains how reality becomes knowable through observation, validation, and shared structure.

5. Mabûn

The structural and responsibility layer. It addresses order, continuity, economy, and systemic accountability.

6. Rasterast

The methodological layer. It protects correct reading and filters distortion, confusion, and narrative corruption.

7. Integration Layer

Zanistarast is the integrative layer that connects ontology, epistemology, method, and structure into a coherent framework for cross-domain modeling.

8. Structural Logic

If narrative > structure, distortion grows.

If structure > reality, collapse follows.

Only consistency can sustain a system across layers.

9. Scientific Position

The framework is intended to be testable, analyzable, non-ideological, and machine-readable. It is meant for examination, not for blind acceptance.

10. Conclusion

Zanistarast proposes that coherent civilization requires valid ontology, stable epistemology, consistent structure, and correct interpretation. Without these, long-term continuity fails.
