Zanistarast Scientific Synthesis

🧠 Theory 📜 Theses 📚 Makaleler ⚙️ Ontology

A structured natural science framework for ontology, epistemology, validation, coherence, and civilizational order.

Abstract

Zanistarast is presented as a structured framework for examining the relation between being, knowledge, validation, and civilizational continuity. It is not framed as ideology or narrative, but as a testable structural model intended to clarify how coherent systems are formed, preserved, and interpreted.

1. Problem

Civilizational collapse begins when knowledge detaches from reality, method detaches from validation, and systems lose structural coherence. Under such conditions, narrative replaces order and fragmentation replaces continuity.

2. Position

Zanistarast takes the position that science must not be adjusted to human preference. Human systems must instead align themselves with reality, structure, and validation. A valid framework therefore begins from ontology, passes through epistemology, and reaches continuity only through disciplined structural coherence.

3. Core Layers

Hebûn — ontological ground of being.

Zanabûn — epistemological organization of knowledge.

Mabûn — structural and responsibility layer.

Rasterast — methodological layer of correct reading and validation.

Zanistarast — integrative synthesis layer.

4. Structural Logic

The system operates under a simple rule set:

If narrative exceeds structure, distortion follows.

If structure breaks from reality, collapse follows.

If validation is absent, continuity becomes impossible.

5. Scientific Aim

The aim is to produce a machine-readable, human-readable, and cross-domain structure capable of comparing concepts, testing claims, and preserving coherence across philosophical, scientific, and civilizational analysis.

6. Civilizational Orientation

The Newroza-Kawa Civilization is presented as the civilizational expression of coherent order when ontology, knowledge, responsibility, and validation are correctly aligned. It is not treated as decorative narrative, but as a structural outcome of valid synthesis.

7. Conclusion

Zanistarast proposes that truth, structure, and consistency are not optional additions to civilization. They are the conditions of its survival. The framework is therefore offered as a natural science synthesis of ontology, knowledge, method, and order.
