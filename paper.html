Zanistarast: A Structural Civilizational Model

Abstract

Zanistarast is a falsifiable, ontology-based civilizational framework integrating ontology, epistemology, structural economy, coherence filtering, and synthesis into a unified system. It proposes that civilizations collapse when knowledge detaches from reality, and that long-term stability depends on structural alignment across layers of existence, validation, responsibility, and integration.

Core Claim

The central claim of the system is that civilizational durability depends on the alignment between reality, knowledge, structure, and coherence. When these layers fragment, instability emerges. When they integrate, durable systems become possible.

Formal Model

Stability = f(Alignment, Coherence, Responsibility, Entropy⁻¹)

In this model, stability increases when coherence and responsibility rise, and decreases when entropy and contradiction dominate systemic relations.

Layer Structure

Hebûn → Ontological Ground
Zanabûn → Epistemic Validation
Mabûn → Structural-Economic Balance
Rasterast → Consistency Filter
Zanistarast → Synthesis Layer
Civilization → Integrated Output

Falsifiability

The framework is not presented as ideology, but as a falsifiable structure. It fails if no measurable relation can be shown between coherence and stability, if structural responsibility has no ordering effect, or if layered alignment produces no meaningful civilizational impact.

Conclusion

Zanistarast is not a closed doctrine. It is a structural model open to criticism, refinement, and testing. Its aim is to move from narrative-centered systems to reality-aligned civilizational architecture.
