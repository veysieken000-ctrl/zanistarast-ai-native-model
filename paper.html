Zanistarast Framework

Zanistarast is a structured, testable framework for modeling natural, computational, and human systems.

1. Introduction

Knowledge is fragmented across domains. Zanistarast proposes a shared structural model to describe how systems emerge, stabilize, and produce outcomes.

2. Layered Architecture

System = f(Root, Validation, Circulation, Functional Alignment) → Outcome
L1 – Root
Origin, base structure, initial condition
L2 – Validation
Consistency, filtering, verification
L3 – Circulation
Flow, transfer, regulated movement
L5 – Functional Alignment
Role execution, harmony, system integrity
L6 – Outcome
Stability, collapse, or transition

3. Testability

The framework is testable. If structural consistency does not hold across domains, the model must be revised.

4. Scope

Applies to structured systems: physical, biological, computational, and human systems.

5. Conclusion

Zanistarast does not claim domains are identical, but that they share structural logic. Its value lies in making this logic explicit and testable.
