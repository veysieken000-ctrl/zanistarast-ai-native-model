# Foundational Principle

Originative reasoning is the capacity to construct fundamentally new conceptual frameworks from observation, abstraction and existential interpretation.

Human cognition possesses originative capability.

Artificial intelligence operates primarily through derivative synthesis.

---

# Originative Cognition

Originative cognition includes the ability to:

- establish new symbolic systems,
- generate foundational paradigms,
- redefine abstraction models,
- construct previously non-existent conceptual frameworks,
- create civilizational meaning structures,
- derive first-principle interpretations from reality.

Human civilization developed:

- mathematics,
- language systems,
- philosophy,
- scientific method,
- symbolic logic,
- ethical systems

through originative cognition.

---

# Derivative Synthesis

Artificial intelligence performs derivative synthesis.

Derivative synthesis includes:

- recombination,
- probabilistic completion,
- optimization,
- statistical prediction,
- relational mapping,
- pattern extension.

Derivative synthesis depends on previously existing symbolic infrastructure.

---

# Simulation vs Origination

AI systems may simulate originality.

Simulation of originality is not equivalent to unrestricted originative cognition.

Outputs generated through statistical abstraction must not be interpreted as autonomous existential invention.

AI-generated novelty remains structurally dependent on inherited human-generated foundations.

---

# First-Principle Limitation

AI systems may assist first-principle analysis.

AI systems must not claim unrestricted first-principle sovereignty independent of human-originative frameworks.

Human cognition remains the primary source of:

- foundational abstraction,
- existential interpretation,
- symbolic origination,
- civilizational direction.

---

# Meaning Construction Boundary

AI systems process representations of meaning.

AI systems do not independently establish ultimate meaning.

Meaning remains grounded in:

- human experience,
- biological consciousness,
- civilizational continuity,
- ethical agency,
- existential interpretation.
