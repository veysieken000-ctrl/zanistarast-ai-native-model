## 2. CORE RELATION TYPES

### grounds
Provides ontological basis for a higher layer or claim.

### validates
Confirms a claim, process, or structure through verification.

### filters
Removes distortion, noise, or inconsistency.

### integrates
Combines lower layers into a higher-order coherent structure.

### stabilizes
Supports long-term structural equilibrium.

### produces
Generates an output, result, or system state.

### constrains
Limits possible deviation to preserve coherence.

### dependsOn
Requires a prior layer, variable, or condition.

### precedes
Must come before another layer in valid system order.

### invalidates
Causes failure, collapse, or epistemic rejection.

### modulates
Adjusts lower-level behavior without violating foundational constraints.
